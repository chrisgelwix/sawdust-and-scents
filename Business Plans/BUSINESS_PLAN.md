## Executive Summary

Sawdust and Scents is an e-commerce venture specializing in handcrafted woodworking products and artisanal candles. The company will operate as a direct-to-consumer (D2C) business, manufacturing products in-house and selling through a proprietary online platform. This business plan outlines our strategy, market opportunity, operational model, and financial projections to achieve profitability and sustainable growth.

### Key Highlights
- **Target Market:** Home decor enthusiasts, gift buyers, and DIY consumers
- **Revenue Model:** Direct sales through e-commerce platform
- **Initial Investment Required:** $150,000 - $200,000
- **Projected Break-Even:** Month 12-15
- **3-Year ROI:** 180-220%
- **Competitive Advantage:** Handcrafted quality, unique designs, direct customer relationships

## 3. Operations Plan

### Production Capacity
- **Initial Setup:** Small workshop (1,000-1,500 sq ft)
- **Equipment:** Woodworking tools, candle-making equipment, packaging station
- **Staffing:** 
  - Year 1: Owner + 1 part-time craftsperson
  - Year 2: Owner + 2 full-time employees
  - Year 3: Owner + 3-4 employees

### Production Workflow
1. **Material Sourcing:** Wood suppliers, wax suppliers, fragrance oils, packaging
2. **Manufacturing:** Batch production for efficiency
3. **Quality Control:** Inspection and finishing
4. **Packaging:** Branded packaging for shipping
5. **Fulfillment:** In-house initially, potential 3PL in Year 2+

### Inventory Management
- **Strategy:** Make-to-order for custom items, maintain stock for popular SKUs
- **Safety Stock:** 30-45 days of inventory for bestsellers
- **Turnover Target:** 6-8 inventory turns per year

### 4.4 Break-Even Analysis

#### Break-Even Point Calculation

**Formula:** Break-Even Units = Fixed Costs / (Selling Price - Variable Cost)

- **Fixed Costs (Monthly):** $8,400
- **Average Selling Price:** $52
- **Average Variable Cost:** $22
- **Contribution Margin per Unit:** $30

**Break-Even Units per Month:** $8,400 / $30 = **280 units**

**Break-Even Revenue per Month:** 280 × $52 = **$14,560**

#### Break-Even Timeline

Based on the revenue projections:
- **Month 9:** First profitable month (300 units sold)
- **Month 12-15:** Cumulative break-even (recovering initial investment losses)
- **Month 18:** Full recovery of Year 1 losses and initial investment

#### Break-Even Sensitivity Analysis

| Scenario | Monthly Units | Monthly Revenue | Break-Even Status |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Conservative (20% lower sales) | 224 | $11,648 | Not reached |
| Base Case | 280 | $14,560 | Break-even |
| Optimistic (20% higher sales) | 336 | $17,472 | Profitable |

### 4.5 Products Made vs. Sold Analysis

#### Production Efficiency Metrics

**Year 1:**
- **Units Produced:** 3,000 (includes safety stock and initial inventory)
- **Units Sold:** 2,710
- **Inventory Turnover:** 2,710 / 290 = 9.3 turns
- **Waste/Unsold Inventory:** 290 units (9.7% of production)
- **Inventory Carrying Cost:** 290 × $22 = $6,380 (written off or sold at discount)

**Year 2:**
- **Units Produced:** 7,000
- **Units Sold:** 6,600
- **Inventory Turnover:** 6,600 / 400 = 16.5 turns
- **Waste/Unsold Inventory:** 400 units (5.7% of production)
- **Inventory Carrying Cost:** 400 × $22 = $8,800

**Year 3:**
- **Units Produced:** 9,500
- **Units Sold:** 9,000
- **Inventory Turnover:** 9,000 / 500 = 18 turns
- **Waste/Unsold Inventory:** 500 units (5.3% of production)
- **Inventory Carrying Cost:** 500 × $22 = $11,000

#### Key Insights
- **Production Efficiency Improves:** Waste percentage decreases from 9.7% to 5.3%
- **Inventory Management:** Safety stock maintained at 30-45 days
- **Make-to-Order Strategy:** Reduces waste for custom/premium items
- **Seasonal Adjustments:** Production scaled based on demand patterns

### 4.6 Return on Investment (ROI) Analysis

#### Investment Summary

#### ROI Calculations

**Simple ROI (3-Year):**
- **Total Return:** $177,000 - $150,000 = $27,000

**Adjusted ROI (Considering Time Value):**
- **Year 2 Return:** $78,000 (discounted)
- **Year 3 Return:** $120,000 (discounted)
- **Net Present Value (NPV) @ 10% discount rate:** ~$15,000

## 6. Risk Analysis & Mitigation

### Key Risks

1. **Market Risk**
   - **Risk:** Slow customer acquisition, competition
   - **Mitigation:** Strong branding, SEO, social media presence, unique product designs

2. **Production Risk**
   - **Risk:** Quality issues, supply chain disruptions
   - **Mitigation:** Quality control processes, multiple supplier relationships, inventory buffers

3. **Financial Risk**
   - **Risk:** Cash flow constraints, slower than projected growth
   - **Mitigation:** Conservative projections, working capital reserve, flexible cost structure

4. **Operational Risk**
   - **Risk:** Key person dependency, scaling challenges
   - **Mitigation:** Documentation, training, gradual team expansion

5. **Technology Risk**
   - **Risk:** E-commerce platform issues, cybersecurity
   - **Mitigation:** Professional development, security best practices, backup systems
